Rocket Nose Cone Development
Along with 2 other engineers of uORocketry, research and
testing went into designing a nose cone, its mould, as well as
selecting its materials and manufacturing process. This nose
cone is for a rocket which has an apogee of 30,000 feet,
travelling at Mach 2.5 and will be competing against other
university teams. Entire CAD and design, excluding the nose
cone tip and bracket was done by myself using SolidWorks.
CFD and consulting was done by all team members.
Automated Wrapping Machine
Along with 4 other students, a machine
designed to automate the process of
wrapping boxes with gift wrap was designed
and modeled using CAD. The cutting
mechanism was designed entirely by myself,
and was designed using a hydraulic cylinder
in order to minimize blade replacement.
Motorized Film Winding Machine Prototype
Wiring, sizing of motor, as well as consultation was done with a client who was
trying to develop a machine designed to wind a roll of plastic film onto a plastic
piece mounted onto a shaft. The parts are 3D printed and were designed by
the client, in order to test the functionality of the system. The machine is still a
work in progress: in the future, proper bearings, and metal parts will be
machined in order to adequately mount the shaft and motor.
Environmental Regulator
Designed especially for the consequences of COVID, the environmental regulator is an
Arduino-based system which has a temperature, humidity, AQI (air-quality index) sensor.
According to the current season and target temperature, this system will open the
window to change the temperature and humidity, improve the AQI, and reduce the
amount of carbon emissions of a room.
